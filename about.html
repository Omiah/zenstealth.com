About Me

Inspired by The Setup

Who are you, and what do you do?

My name is Adith Radityo, and I'm a high school student. I like to experiment with programming and web design in the brief moments of spare time that I have in my hectic school life. On the internet I go by the alias zenstealth.

I love four things: technology, science, minimalism, and indie rock.

What hardware are you using?

Right now I use MacBook from 2007. While it might be an old computer, it still gets the job done and does it better than any PC I've ever used. I plan to get a newer Mac when I go off to college. I'm also in the process of building a gaming PC from scratch.

I have two external hard drives: a 1.5TB Western Digital WD Elements and an ordinary 160GB HDD stuffed into an external enclosure. On both of them I a fully bootable copy of my MacBook, backed up via Carbon Copy Cloner.

For a keyboard, I use a Dell AT101W. It's old, it's mechanical, and it's an absolute dream to type on.There's an awesome story behind this keyboard: while helping out at the back of my school's library one day, I noticed it on a desk covered with other old computer stuff that was about to be thrown away. I picked it up and checked to see if it was working - which it did. I then asked the Principal if I can keep it, and he said yes. Long story short, I saved a kick-ass mechanical keyboard from utter destruction, and in return I got it for free. I was so happy that day,

And what software?

I'm a OS X kind of guy, but I like experimenting with Linux from time to time. My favorite distro is Ubuntu - I happen to carry a portable installation of Ubuntu with me on my 8GB USB drive - It's very convenient to have your own fully customized bootable operating system everywhere you go.

Whenever I'm on my MacBook, Terminal.app is always running and is easily accessible via a hotkey thanks to Visor. When I'm in a 1337 h4xx0r kind of mood at school, I like to fool my friends into thinking I am one by running iTerm2 and configuring it so it'll look like this. I know how to use Vim, just for the heck of it. Once I know how to properly use it, I'll be switching to that as my primary code editor.

Firefox is my browser of choice, because it is the only browser that I can customize to just the way I like - here is my current Firefox config. As you can see, it maximizes the usage of vertical space to the fullest extent possible.

I use Git to manage my code, and I use GitHub to store my repositories.
This website is powered by Jekyll - a Ruby powered static site generator.

What would be your dream setup?

Short of computer chips implanted in my brain along with robotic limbs, my dream setup would be a 15" MacBook Air that is as powerful as a fully-loaded Mac Pro. It would be powered by a tiny fuel cell so that I won't have to recharge it every 7 hours or so, and instead of traditional ports, it'll have some sort of localized high-speed wireless connectivity (like Bluetooth combined with Thunderbolt) so that you can wirelessly connect computer monitors and any other wireless peripherals like a keyboard and mouse to it.
