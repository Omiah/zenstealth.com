About Me

Inspired by The Setup

Who are you, and what do you do?

My name is Adith Radityo, and I'm a high school student. I like to experiment with code and web design in the brief moments of spare time that I have in my hectic school life. I love four things: technology, science, minimalism, and indie rock.

What hardware are you using?

Right now I use an old 2007 MacBook. While it might not be as powerful as a MacBook Pro (or even a current generation MacBook), it gets the job done and does it better than any PC I have ever used. Even though I don't like PCs that much, right now I'm in the process of building one from scratch so I can play all my Windows only games.

And what software?

I love using Mac OS X, and I like to experimenting with Linux time to time with Ubuntu. I carry a portable installation of Ubuntu with me on my 8GB USB drive for emergencies - I'm a bit paranoid about using other people's computers. The only time I would ever use Windows is when I'm forced to use it (like on some school computers), or when I want to play my Windows only video games

Firefox is my browser of choice, because it's still a lot more customizable than Google Chrome. I love tweaking Firefox to get it just the way I want it to - here is a screenshot of my current Firefox config right now. As you can see, it maximizes the usage of vertical space the fullest extent possible.

I use Git to manage my code, and I use GitHub to store my repositories.

What would be your dream setup?

My dream setup would be a 15" MacBook Air that is as powerful as a fully-loaded Mac Pro. It would be powered by a tiny fuel cell so that I won't have to recharge it every 7 hours or so, and instead of traditional ports, it'll have some sort of localized high-speed wireless connectivity (like Bluetooth combined with Thunderbolt) so that you can wirelessly connect computer monitors and any other wireless peripherals like a keyboard and mouse to it.
