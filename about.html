About Me

Inspired by The Setup

Who are you, and what do you do?

Hello, my name is Adith Radityo, and currently I'm a student. On the internet I go by the alias zenstealth. I like technology, science, minimalism, and indie rock. In my free time I like to play the guitar and mess around with things like soldering and other hardware related activities.

What hardware are you using?

I use a Macbook (Mid-2007). It's completely maxed out with a 2GHz Intel 2 Core Duo, 4GB of RAM, a 750GB HDD. For a 5 year old laptop I still can't believe how well it performs everyday; it plays 1080p mkv videos and 720p flash videos smoothly, and it even handles a heavily customized Firefox profile just fine.

I use a mechanical Dell AT101W as my keyboard. I rescued it from a computer junk pile at the back of my school's library, and I was granted permission to keep it.

I always carry around my rooted LG Optimus T with me, along with a 30GB iPod video 5G and a Monoprice MHP-839 headphone.

I like reading books a lot, so I also carry my Kindle Touch with me so I can read when I have some downtime.

I have a Raspberry Pi, the 512MB Model B version, which I mainly use as a server/robot butler. Sometimes I use it as a wearable computer that I hook up to an LCD eye display.

And what software?

  • I use OS X 10.7 Lion (the highest supported version for the MacBook), and at any given moment Terminal is always running and is easily accessible via a hotkey thanks to Visor. I have TotalFinder installed which enables tabbed browsing, cut & paste, and many other useful features that Apple should have implemented long ago in Finder.
  • Firefox is my browser of choice. I use it because I like how heavily customizable Firefox is, and how it's the only browser with bookmark keyword support done right. I use Git for version control, and I use GitHub to store my repositories. This website is powered by Jekyll.
  • Sublime Text is my text editor of choice on the Mac, along with Vim, TextEdit, and Mou.
  • Evernote is where I store all my notes, and it essentially acts as my second brain. You store notes in it, which later is tagged, indexed, and synced to the cloud for easy retrieval and safekeeping. You can read more on The Verge about how Evernote will make your life more efficient.
  • iTunes is how I manage my music library, and every song or album in it is tagged with the proper metadata and is usually included the high-res version of the album cover. Whenever I get new music I try find the lossless version of it because I do care about audio quality when I listen to music.
  • When using Linux (GNU/Linux or GNU plus Linux or whatever you like to call it) I prefer Xubuntu because it has the same features and compatibility of Ubuntu without the atrocious Unity interface and it doesn't send your search data to third parties in order to make money. For servers I like to use Debian, and Raspbian for the Raspberry Pi.

What would be your dream setup?

My realistic dream setup is a MacBook Pro with Retina Display, painted completely black. It'd be partnered alongside a custom-built gaming computer that's connected to five - IPS monitors.

My near-future dream setup (for at home) will be an Oculus Rift and a pair of haptic-feedback gloves so I can interact with things inside a Second Life type virtual reality - just like in the book READY PLAYER ONE.

Also, I'll probably be wearing a pair of Google Glasses all the time. I'll attach an external sensor array to it so I can see in infrared, radar, and sonar.

My far-into-the-future-yet-totally-possible dream setup is:

  • Brain-Computer Interface Chip
  • Wireless 24/7 internet connection (with a hardwired off-switch).
  • Artificial eyes with optical zoom, thermal vision, and heads up display interface.
  • Respirocytes. You have no idea how useful these little oxygen-carrying guys will be when you're in a tough situation.
