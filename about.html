About Me

Inspired by The Setup

Who are you, and what do you do?

My name is Adith Radityo, and I'm a high school student. I like to experiment with programming and web design in the brief moments of spare time that I have in my hectic school life. On the internet I go by the alias zenstealth.

I like technology, science, minimalism, and indie rock.

What hardware are you using?

Right now I use MacBook from 2007. It has a 2GHz Inter 2 Core Duo, 4GB of RAM, a 750GB HDD, and Intel GMA 950 64MB - surprisingly it's still the smoothest computer I've ever had. I'm also in the process of building a gaming PC from scratch.

For a keyboard, I use a Dell AT101W. Funny story about that keyboard - I found it in my school's pile of junk behind the media center and asked the principal if I could have it (he said yes). To this day I still can't believe I got a keyboard as awesome as this for free.

I have three external hard drives: a 1.5TB Western Digital WD Elements, an ordinary 160GB HDD stuffed into an external enclosure, and a 2.5" 20GB HDD gutted from an Xbox 360 drive. On two of them I a fully bootable copy of my MacBook. My backup policy is to ALWAYS have a backup of your backup, and it's also a good idea to have a backup for your backup's backup.

And what software?

I use OS X 10.7 Lion, and at any given moment Terminal.app is always running and is easily accessible via a hotkey thanks to Visor. I have TotalFinder installed which enables tabbed browsing, cut & paste, and many other useful features that Apple should have implemented long ago in Finder.

I like to experiment with Linux from time to time. My favorite Linux distro is Debian.

Firefox is my browser of choice, because it is the only browser that I can customize to just the way I like it, and it has the only proper implementation of bookmark keywords anywhere. Here is my current Firefox config.

MacVim is my text editor of choice. I use Git to manage my code, and I use GitHub to store my repositories. This website is powered by Jekyll.

What would be your dream setup?

Short of being Adam Jensen (below), my dream setup would be a MacBook Pro with Retina Display that is painted completely matte black and has a nuclear fusion reactor. I also want a pair of Google Glasses.
