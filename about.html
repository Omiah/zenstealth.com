About Me

Inspired by The Setup

Who are you, and what do you do?

My name is Adith Radityo, and I'm currently a student. On the internet I go by the alias zenstealth. I like technology, science, minimalism, and indie rock.

What hardware are you using?

For now I use MacBook from 2007. It has a 2GHz Intel 2 Core Duo, 4GB of RAM, a 750GB HDD. For a 5 year old laptop, it still is the smoothest computer I've ever owned; it can handle a fully loaded Firefox install, a 720p FLASH YouTube video, a Twitter client, an FTP client, iTunes, 3 different text editors running all at the same time.

For a keyboard, I use a mechanical Dell AT101W, which was given to me for free for volunteering at the school library.

I have three external hard drives: a 1.5TB Western Digital WD Elements, an ordinary 160GB HDD stuffed into an external enclosure, and a 2.5" 20GB HDD gutted from an Xbox 360 drive. On two of them I a fully bootable copy of my MacBook. My backup policy is to ALWAYS have a backup of your backup, and it's also a good idea to have a backup for your backup's backup.

I always carry around my rooted LG Optimus T with me, along with a 30GB iPod video and a Monoprice MHP-839 headphone for listening to music on the go.

I prefer to read books on my Kindle Touch rather than on a physical paper book because of how convenient it is being able to carry a lot of books in a very light and thin device - which is very useful inside a heavily stuffed backpack.

And what software?

I use OS X 10.7 Lion, and at any given moment Terminal.app is always running and is easily accessible via a hotkey thanks to Visor. I have TotalFinder installed which enables tabbed browsing, cut & paste, and many other useful features that Apple should have implemented long ago in Finder.

Firefox is my browser of choice. I like how heavily customizable Firefox is, and how it's the only browser with bookmark keyword support built-in.

TextEdit is my text editor of choice on the Mac, along with Vim. Mou is great for editing my Markdown powered blog posts. I use Git to manage my code, and I use GitHub to store my repositories. This website is powered by Jekyll.

I like to experiment with Linux from time to time. My favorite Linux distributions are Debian and Xubuntu.

What would be your dream setup?

Short of being an augmented super-human cyborg like Adam Jensen, my dream setup would be a MacBook Pro with Retina Display that is painted completely matte black, that has at least 1 week's worth of battery life.

I also want a pair of Google Glasses.
