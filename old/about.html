About Me

Who are you, and what do you do?

My name is Adith Radityo, and I'm a high school student. I like to experiment with code and web design in the brief moments of spare time that I have in my hectic school life. I love four things: technology, science, minimalism, and indie rock.

What do you plan to do with your life?

I don't quite know yet. In contrast to the stuff that the consumerist culture I live in wants me to do (buy a lot of stuff, get into debt, work so you can buy more stuff, repeat until death), I plan to get a job that I enjoy doing and pays just enough for me to live my life comfortably in a small house that is filled all sorts of technology (obviously).

Caution: very geeky stuff up ahead.

What kind of hardware do you use?

Right now I use an old 2007 MacBook. While it might not be as powerful as a MacBook Pro (or even a current generation MacBook), it gets the job done and does it better than any PC I have ever used. Even though I don't like PCs that much, right now I'm in the process of building one from scratch so I can play all my Windows only games.

And what about software?

I love using Mac OS X, and I like to experimenting with Linux time to time with Ubuntu. I carry a portable installation of Ubuntu with me on my 8GB USB drive for emergencies - I'm a bit paranoid about using other people's computers. The only time I would ever use Windows is when I'm forced to use it (like on some school computers), or when I want to play my Windows only video games.

Firefox is my browser of choice, because it's still a lot more customizable than Google Chrome. As you can see, it maximizes the usage of vertical space the fullest extent possible.

I use Git to manage my code, and I use GitHub to store my repositories. Here is the repository for this website, in case you're interested.

What would be your dream setup?

My dream setup would be a computer chip installed into my brain which uses the optic nerve to display a HUD (Head-up Display) right in front of me. It would be hooked up wirelessly to the internet, and be powered by the human body itself. I also want some robotic arms and legs.

If you have ever seen the Ghost in the Shell series, or played the game Deus Ex, you know what I'm talking about.
