About Me

Inspired by The Setup

Who are you, and what do you do?

Hello, my name is Adith Radityo, and I'm a student. On the internet I go by the alias zenstealth. I like technology, science, minimalism, and indie rock. In my free time I like to play the guitar and mess around with things like soldering and other hardware related activities.

What hardware are you using?

My main computer is a MacBook from mid-2007, and it's completely maxed out with a 2GHz Intel 2 Core Duo, 4GB of RAM, a 750GB HDD. This is the first computer I have ever bought, and I paid for it with my first paycheck - this machine is my pride and joy. For a 5-year-old laptop I still can't believe how well it still performs; it plays 1080p videos smoothly, it streams 720p YouTube videos just fine, and it even handles a heavily customized Firefox profile very gracefully. One day I'll replace the disk drive with an SSD.

Sometimes I use a Dell AT101W as my keyboard. It's mechanical, and I got the thing for free by rescuing it from a computer junk pile at the back of my school's library.

The gadgets I always carry with me are my rooted LG Optimus T, a 30GB 5th generation iPod video with a Monoprice MHP-839 headphone, and a Kindle Touch.

I am a proud and lucky owner of a 512MB Model B Raspberry Pi, which I mainly use as a server. Sometimes I use it as a wearable computer that I hook up to a LCD eye display.

And what software?

Lion is the highest supported version for the MacBook so that's what I'm stuck with. At any given moment Terminal is always running and easily hidden yet accessible via a hotkey thanks to Visor. I have TotalFinder installed which enables tabbed browsing, cut & paste, and many other useful features that Apple should have implemented long ago in Finder.

Firefox is my browser of choice and I use it all the time because of how customizable Firefox is, and how it's the only browser with bookmark keywords done right. Evernote is how I store all my notes, and it essentially acts as my second brain. It works like magic: you add notes to it, and it's later is tagged, indexed, and synced to the cloud for easy retrieval and safekeeping.

Sublime Text is my text editor of choice on the Mac, along with Vim, TextEdit, and Mou. I use Git for version control, and I use GitHub to store my repositories. This website is powered by Jekyll.

iTunes is how I manage my music library, and every song or album in it is tagged with the proper metadata and is usually includes the high-res version of the album cover. I like getting new songs as lossless formats, and I use XLD to convert them into the Apple friendly ALAC format. VLC is the best video player ever, since it plays just about every format known to man.

When using Linux (GNU/Linux or GNU plus Linux or whatever you like to call it) I prefer Xubuntu because it has the same features and compatibility of Ubuntu without the atrocious Unity interface and it doesn't send your search data to third parties in order to make money. For servers I like to use Debian, and Raspbian for the Raspberry Pi.

What would be your dream setup?

My realistic dream setup is a 15" Retina MacBook Pro, painted completely black. It'd be partnered with a custom-built dedicated gaming PC that's connected to at least five IPS resolution monitors and a surround system.

My near-future dream setup (for at home) will be an Oculus Rift and a pair of haptic-feedback gloves so I can interact with things inside a Second Life-type virtual reality, just like in Ernest Cline's amazing book READY PLAYER ONE.

Also, I'll be wearing a rooted pair of Google Glasses which I'll name Sebastian because I really want to change the default command from "Okay Glass" to "Sebastian this is an order". Later on I'll eventually attach an external sensor array in the future so I can see stuff in infrared, radar, and sonar.

My far-into-the-future-yet-still-physically-possible dream setup is:

  • Brain-Computer Interface Chip.
  • Wireless 24/7 internet connection (with a hardwired off-switch).
  • Mechanical arms and legs capable of superhuman strength.
  • A spine upgrade will probably be needed for those arms.
  • Artificial eyes with optical zoom, thermal vision, and heads up display interface.
  • Respirocytes. You have no idea how useful these little oxygen-carrying guys will be when you're in a tough situation.
